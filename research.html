Big Themes

During my Ph.D., I have focused my research on using the latest advances in machine learning combined with traditional techniques to study the formation & evolution of galaxies, and investigate the specific role played by Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) in this process.

With regards to machine learning, I have focused on building models that a) can accurately account for different uncertainties in their predictions; b) are interpretable; c) can be trained with minimal real training data; d) can be applied to data over a varying range of redshift, signal-to- noise ration, and resolution. Our algorithms have brought novel features into the astro-informatics domain and greatly increased our abilitly to harness new scientific output from existing and upcoming large datasets. All my tools and trained models are publicly available with along with extensive documentation and tutorials.

I have used my algorithms primarily to determine morphological parameters (along with robust uncertainties) for optical morphologies of galaxies and AGN. With our frameworks, we have produced previously unknown morphologies for $\sim 8$ Million galaxies and AGN in the Hyper Suprime-Cam Survey. Our algorithms have also been applied to SDSS and CANDELS. Since morphology is connection to various fundamental properties of the galaxy and it's environment, we have harnessed this morphological information to a) identify different evolutionary pathways for galaxies at different redshifts; b) delineate the role played by AGN in this process; c) gain insight into the morphological predictions made by semi-analytical models and cosmological simulations.
